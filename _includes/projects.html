Projects

Productive 4.0 - A major European H2020/ECSEL initiative with 109 partners aimed at advancing Europe's leadership in the Digital Industry through enhanced IoT integration and digitalization across the value chain. My role was centered within Work Package 4 (WP4), titled 'Process Virtualization'. In this capacity, I focused on the development and application of complex, agent-based simulation models to analyze, virtualize, and optimize industrial processes, with a specific emphasis on semiconductor supply chains. Key responsibilities included utilizing data analysis techniques to parameterize and interpret these sophisticated simulations , applying simulation insights to improve Advanced Planning & Scheduling (APS) systems within the supply chain context , and contributing to the practical implementation of Digital Twin concepts for enhanced process management. This work involved collaboration with major industry partners, such as Bosch, particularly on developing standardized data models essential for effective simulation and planning in the semiconductor sector.
hap - An Android application developed with Xamarin, designed to manage and visualize user-defined events called Drops. It features a clean, modular architecture following the Model-View-Controller pattern, enabling efficient local data handling, image management, and synchronization with external data sources. The app offers intuitive interfaces for creating, filtering, and viewing event details, providing a seamless user experience.
